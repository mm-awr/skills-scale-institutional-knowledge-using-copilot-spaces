# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

OctoAcme uses a lightweight project lifecycle designed to move from idea to measurable outcomes with clear ownership and decision points. Work starts in Initiation by defining the problem, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, and early risks in a one-pager and related kickoff artifacts. Once priorities and team availability are aligned, the project moves into Planning to break scope into shippable increments, define acceptance criteria, estimate work, and document dependencies, release milestones, and Definition of Done.

Execution is managed through a consistent delivery rhythm and visible workflow states. Teams track work on a board using **Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done**, supported by daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and sprint or milestone demos. Pull requests are expected to be small when possible, linked to issues with acceptance criteria, validated by CI checks, and reviewed before merge according to team policy.

Roles and communication practices are explicit to keep cross-functional work coordinated. Project Managers drive schedules, risks, dependencies, and stakeholder updates; Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize backlog decisions; Developers and QA partner on implementation quality and acceptance validation; and stakeholders provide direction and approvals. Communication follows a regular cadence with weekly or milestone-based updates and a single source of truth for status, while escalation paths move from team triage to PM, Product Lead, and Sponsor when business impact increases.

Quality, release, and learning are handled as part of the delivery system rather than one-time events. OctoAcme expects unit, integration, and critical-flow smoke testing (plus CI security scanning), then uses a release checklist that includes readiness criteria, release notes, and rollback planning before and after deployment verification. Retrospectives after sprints, milestones, releases, and incidents capture what worked, what needs improvement, and turn top insights into owned, time-bound follow-up actions.
